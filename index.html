The Project

The Goal

The goal is to capture the stories of people who have interacted with the Sheela-na-gig and especially those with a spiritual element.

Your Rights

The story you tell belongs toyou – always and forever. It is posted on this site with your permission. If you change your mind, it will be deleted.

Who Profits?

We all profit -spiritually and intellectually. This is an academic project. No money is paid for the stories. The stories are not sold for profit.

Concordia

The project is affiliated with the Centre for Oral History and Digital Storytelling Project at Concordia University in Montreal, Canada

Sheela-na-gigs

The Sheela-na-gig is a medieval stone carving of a naked female figure with a pose that emphasizes her genitalia. The carvings are found on churches, castles, and monasteries and are mainly located in Ireland. The original meaning of the Sheela image remains unknown. In more recent times, however, it has been used as a feminist icon, talisman, fertility figure and focus of devotional rituals.

Sonya Ocampo-Gooding

Born in Cali, Colombia and obtained a Master of Fine Arts degree in the Special Individualised Program (now INDI) at Concordia University, Montreal. An art historian who specializes in Irish art with an interdisciplinary approach as seeing art not simply through form and colour, but understanding how historical, religious, social forces and oral histories drive the creative process. I have presented at “New Crops, New Fields: (Re) Imagining Irish Folklore” at Queens University, Belfast and will be at the “Hypothesis Symposia” at the Montreal Museum of Fine Arts next March 2015. Being published in Ireland and Canada.
